                              [head=3]Species Report: "Shadekin"[/head]

Written by: [bold][ERROR[e]][/bold], NT Representative 
Date: 8-10-2200
TO: Trans-Solar Federation

[head=4]Physical Properties[/head]
    Shadekin seem to be a dog-like humanoid species with 4 ears and a whale-like tail. Their eyes are bright and expressive but still have a lot of color variety that is similar to mammalian species. They're entire body is covered with a single color, but the color is varied across individuals.

    However, they are also incredibly light sensitive and symptoms relating to that will grow as the luminosity increases, integrated over time above an unknown luminosity. 
    
    
[head=4]Arrival And Housing[/head]
    After the Shadekin docked with their self-piloted space-fairing shuttle, the AI alerted us to their activity. Before the Shadekin even came out, we provided a room set to the same air contents, brightness, and temperature of the shuttle. The doors to the main station are double-airlocked to keep this environment stable. As

    Before being able to leave or enter the room, we wore bio suits for the fist week while collecting air samples and other stuff that the Shadekin gave to us in a bag for pathogen analysis. The Shadekin also showed no fear to the bio suits and nodded after seeing them. 
    
    The station provided the Shadekin with a mechanical version of the cargo supply computer, a headset with common comms (taught how to use it.) The only time we came in was when there was a documented medical emergency, a delivery, or if the Shadekin invited us in. Though, the only exception was a doorbell we had installed before letting the Shadekin onto the station. We also where always allowed into the meeting room, as the Shadekin always opened the door for us.

    The Shadekin ended up expanding the allocated area and added a variety of rooms. This was approved on our part, all of the local station command, Centcom, and NT in under 5 days with a formal construction permit that allows the Shadekin to do any construction within, or to expand the living areas if it does not interfere with civilians. The Shadekin mostly respected this other then one instance of empathy-related impulsiveness.

    
•  A meeting room that was connected to the airlocks leading to the rest of the station
• They also made a room that was completely dark with a bed in it. 
• The shuttle was almost entirely stripped of internal non-necessities and moved onto the main station. 
• (the Shadekin is the only one who constructed this) A medbay with a minimum amount of light that seemed to cause some discomfort, but not enough to actively make them burn. They seemingly did this so we could treat any injuries that could have shown up. 


    After the week of legally required quarantine time, where nothing was found, the Shadekin was allowed to open the doors at any time.
    

[head=4]Language[/head]
    The language of this species seems to be a mono-worded language that works only based on tone. The word is most similar to how "Marr" is pronounced in Galactic Common.

    We have not yet decoded their language as of this time, but they seem to be highly intelligent. 

    Though, within the first week, the Shadekin already seemingly understood some of the more commonly used words in Galatic Common. The Shadekin also had a perfect understanding of some of the gestures that humans use to communicate non verbally. 

[head=4]Empathy[/head]
    Before the first week was over the the Shadekin showed signs of high levels of empathy to our coworker after his wife had died. The Shadekin then, in an impulsive matter, methodically deconstructed airlocks that lead into areas we had not prepared.  The Shadekin did not attempt to break in further, nor escape after reaching the coworker. Vital signs from the coworker's jumpsuit reported that the Shadekin was incredibly calming (coworker had consented to us pulling his biomedical records in this case). He also reported that they purred like cats. After calming the coworker down, the Shadekin then returned to their quarters, while replacing the doors that they deconstructed. This shows that they respect the separation of areas because of biological needs. The coworker thoroughly decontaminated himself of any potential contaminants as that is part of the requirements for potential infections.  

    Those doors are incredibly tough to deconstruct even for our engineers with power tools. 

    On top of that, this was the first recorded instance of the Shadekin trying to break out of its room and extended  environment. 

[head=4]Writing[/head]
    After about a week, we discovered the Shadekin writing in a journal in a meeting room that both the Shadekin and researchers could go into. We are glad that they can write. Because of that, I personally hope we can get a linguistic expert on our team before we send this out to the TSF.

    Obviously, it would be good research data if we knew what the journal said, but we don't understand the language, and it is also a personal journal so it wouldn't happen until we actually understand the language for consent. The Shadekin s handed to us open and was pointing at parts of the script. Though, we gave it back because we can't do anything with it. Once we can actually understand the language, we plan to ask, and then study the journal if consent is given.
